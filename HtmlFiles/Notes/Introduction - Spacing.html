Spacing

Although the K-Function is a popular tool for assessing interpoint interaction, additional information about a point pattern can often be revealed by measuring spacings or shortest distances in the pattern, such as the 'nearest-neighbour' distances from each data point to the nearest other data point. Furthermore, there is a duality between measuring shortest distances between points and counting points in a given area. Consider for example, you may be interested in the spatial distribution of air/drone strikes in an area surrounding a particular city. In one way, you may look and find that there are on average 2 air/drone strikes within a 10 mile radius of a air/drone strike occuring in the city. In another way, you may find that on average a drone strike occurs roughly 5 miles between every air/drone strike. These statements are not necessarily equal, but convey similar information about the spatial distribution of air/drone strikes.

What does it tell us?

The nearest neighbour distance distribution function and empty space function are used to assess interpoint interaction (spatial correlation) just as the K-Function does, but it does it by using the distances between points, instead of counting the number of r-neighbours a point has.

Measuring Distances

There are several ways to measure distances in a point pattern.

  • Nearest Neighbour Distances: The distance from a point to its nearest neighbour.
  • The empty-space distance: The distance from a fixed reference location u in the window to the nearest data point.
